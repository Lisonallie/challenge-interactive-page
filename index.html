Summary

Big open skies are a wonderful feature of our countryside and on a clear night you can see over 4,000 stars sparkling in our universe. So, why not follow Galileo Galilei's footsteps?

Join me for a short stroll on level paths on Sussex's highest point to explore the night sky. Away from the light pollution of nearby towns, Black Downâ€™s sweeping skies and unspoilt, expansive views across five counties make it an ideal spot for observing the stars.

As well as hearing the churring of Nightjars and seeing Pippestrelle bats hunting insects, we should see the Milky Way, the Summer Triangle and Polaris before the star of the evening arrives- the Perseid meteor shower. Tonight they are at their peak, sending 60 or more shooting stars per hour across the night sky. With a favourable Moon, we should be in for quite a show. It's visible with the naked eye, it's amazing and it's free!
